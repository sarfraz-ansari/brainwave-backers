What is Dementia?

Dementia is a term used to describe a group of symptoms affecting memory, thinking, and social abilities severely enough to interfere with daily life. It is caused by damage to brain cells, which affects their ability to communicate with each other. Common symptoms include memory loss, confusion, and difficulty with communication. Dementia is not a single disease but a general term that encompasses various conditions, including Alzheimer’s disease.

Learn More

What causes dementia?

Dementia is primarily caused by damage to brain cells, which affects their ability to communicate. Common causes include Alzheimer’s disease, vascular issues, and conditions like Parkinson’s disease. Other factors such as head injuries, infections, and certain genetic disorders can also contribute. Lifestyle factors like poor diet and lack of exercise may increase the risk.

Early Prediction Benefits

Early prediction of dementia offers several benefits. It allows for timely intervention and treatment, which can slow disease progression. Early diagnosis also helps in planning for future care and making important decisions while the person is still able. Additionally, it provides access to support services and reduces anxiety by providing clarity about the condition.
